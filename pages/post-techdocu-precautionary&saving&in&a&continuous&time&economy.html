Precautionary saving in a continuous time economy

This blog gives a toy example about how to analyze the precautionary saving in continuous time economies. It is for illustration only.

Setup

Suppose the economy starts from t=0. Consider a household who maximizes his/her lifetime utility by choose ct which is a function of time t:

(1)maxc[0,∞)E∫0∞e−ρtlog⁡ctdts.t. dat=rat+zt−ctdzt=ξ(z¯−zt)dt+σdWt

where at is the holding of liquid asset, zt that follows an Ornstein–Uhlenbeck process is the income uncertainty, and Wt is a one-dimension Brownian motion. Let (a0,z0) be the initial condition of the system.

Before giving its stochastic maximum principle, one may notice that:

  • Time homogeneous

  • The volatility σu is independent from the control ct

    • ⟹ No second-order adjoint pair needed

    • ⟹ Maximum condition simplified to maximizing the Hamiltonian

  • The feasible set of ct is convex and we can assume that ct is C1

    • ⟹ Extended stochastic Hamiltonian system

Now, let’s go. Firstly, let’s define Hamiltonian:

(2)H(at,zt;ct;p→t,q→t):=log⁡ct+pa,t⋅(rat+zt−ct)+pz,t⋅ξ(z¯−zt)+qa,t⋅0+qz,t⋅σ=log⁡ct+pa,t⋅(rat+zt−ct)+pz,t⋅ξ(z¯−zt)+qz,t⋅σ

where p→t:=(pa,t,pz,t)∈R2 and q→t:=[qa,tqaz,tqza,tqz,t]∈R2×2 are the first-order adjoint variables.

One may notice that only qz,t is used. Because zt is the only uncertainty source of the system.

Then, let’s write down the stochastic Hamiltonian system as a FBSDE:

(3)ct=1/pa,tdat=(rat+zt−ct)dtdzt=ξ(z¯−zt)dt+σdWtdpa,t=pa,t(ρ−r)dtdpz,t=[ρpz,t−(pa,t−pz,t)]dt+qz,tdWtlimt→∞e−ρtpa,tat=0

Combining the 1st and the 4th SDE, we can get the Euler equation:

(4)ct=1pa,tcitedct=d(1pa,t)dctct=pa,t⋅d(1pa,t)applying Ito’s lemmadctct=pa,t⋅{[0+pa,t(ρ−r)(−1pa,t2)+0]dt+0⋅dWt}dctct=(r−ρ)dt

FYI: cp. the Euler equation of a discrete time counterpart: E{ctct+1|zt}=1+ρ1+r

FYI: One may notice that the SDE about the shadow value pa,t degenerates to an ODE about time t. Thus, here we can take log on both sides of the 1st condition then take time derivatives. This is a common trick in deterministic economies.

FYI: This Euler equation is separable so we can solve the dynamics of the optimal consumption: ct+Δt=cte(r−ρ)Δt

If we look at a deterministic economy by shutting down zt to any constant (typically z¯), we can get a similar Euler equation c˙t/ct=r−ρ.

Size of the precautionary saving

In discrete time models, we measure the precautionary saving motive by prudence but it is usually hard to measure the size of precautionary saving. But this could be done in a continuous time setting under specific settings.

Let Δt be a small enough time period. Given state (at,zt) at time t, we define the saving (as a random variable) from time t to time t+Δt:

(5)Δat+Δt:=at+Δt−at=∫tt+Δt(ras+zs−cs)ds≈rat⋅Δt−∫tt+Δtcsds+∫tt+Δtzsds

Because of log utility, the consumption is a fraction of at such that ct=ρat. Thus,

(6)∫tt+Δtcsds≈ρat⋅Δt

FYI: this property allows us to properly compare this model and its deterministic counterpart.

Let’s do trapezoid approximation:

(7)∫tt+Δtzsds≈Δt2(zt+zt+Δt)=12[2zt+(zt+Δt−zt)]⋅Δt

FYI: strictly, we need the continuity property of OU process to perform the above approximation.

Let ZΔt:=zt+Δt−zt be income growth. By the property of OU process, this income growth follows a normal distribution:

(8)ZΔt∼N(ξ(z¯−zt)⋅Δt,σ2⋅Δt)

Plugging the above approximations back to the saving:

(9)Δat+Δt≈(r−ρ)atΔt+ztΔt+12Δt⋅ZΔt=[(r−ρ)at+zt]⋅Δt+12ZΔt⋅Δt

which follows a normal distribution:

(10)Δat+Δt∼N(μt,Δta,Σt,Δta)μt,Δta:=[(r−ρ)at+wzt]⋅Δt+12ξ(μ−zt)⋅ΔtΣt,Δta:=σ24(Δt)3

To measure the size of precautionary saving, we need to compare Δat+Δt with its deterministic counterpart. Let Δat+Δtdeter be the saving amount in such an economy without uncertainty (the derivation is left for practice). One can get:

(11)Δat+Δtdeter≈[(r−ρ)at+wzt]⋅Δt

Thus, we can define a statistics of the expected size of precautionary saving:

(12)EtΔat+Δt−Δat+Δtdeter≈12ξ(z¯−zt)Δt

The interpretation is that: when facing a bad income shock (zt<z¯), the household, cp. with a deterministic world, extraly saves a fraction of the expected income growth.
